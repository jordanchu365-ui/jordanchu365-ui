Rear Bike Brake Caliper

Year2025
RoleDesigner
ToolsSiemens NX · FEA · SLS

Objective

Design and optimize a rear brake caliper that met ISO 4210-2 and ISO 4210-4 braking standards: brake within 10 m traveling at 25 km/h.

Design Considerations

Before any designs were modeled, the forces exerted onto the calipers — values that would eventually dictate their size and geometry — were analytically estimated using the bike's metrics along with values determined by ISO standards.

Assuming a velocity of 25 km/h, a desired braking distance of 10 m, a total mass of 100 kg, and a coefficient of friction of 0.9, the normal force was calculated to be 267.6 N, corresponding to a frictional force of 240.8 N. These values, along with the ISO standard hand-brake cable force of 180 N, would serve as the forces exerted onto the calipers for the initial designs.

To analyze the maximum working space the calipers can have, initial sketches of the overall design space of both calipers were used to model the first iterations. An interlocking design, where the left caliper would fit into the right, was chosen to minimize the torsional stresses caused by the friction forces.

FEA was then performed on both design spaces using a cable and friction force of 180 N and 240.8 N, respectively, confirming that these chunky, initial designs, if made of Nylon 12, were more than capable of withstanding the exerted forces.

These simulations confirmed that the current designs experienced minimal stress and displacement from the exerted forces, allowing room for sleeker designs that minimized weight. Topology optimization was then performed on both design spaces to give insight into where material can be cut when maximizing stiffness and optimizing load paths.

FEA was then performed on these refined (V1) models to confirm they would be able to withstand all the forces exerted on them.

Fatigue & Safety Analysis

Using Basquin's Law and a safety factor of 1.5, allowable stress for fatigue over 3,650 cycles (10 brakes/day, 365 days/year, 1 year) was 26.48 MPa. This safety factor was chosen for all design parameters because it provides a reasonable margin for uncertainties such as material defects during the SLS printing process, while also prioritizing a lightweight caliper design without the use of excess material. Since 26.48 MPa was less than the maximum allowable stress due to static failure (33.33 MPa, using a safety factor of 1.5, since the σUTS of Nylon 12 is 50 MPa), it would serve as the maximum allowable stress experienced by both calipers. Additionally, using a safety factor of 1.5, the maximum total deflection was calculated to be 7.33 mm (11 mm / 1.5).

The left and right calipers exhibited a maximum stress of 21 and 18 MPa, respectively, and a maximum deflection of 1.8 mm and 1.54 mm, respectively (total of 3.34 mm), all of which are well below the maximum allowable values.
