Projects / FSAE Undertray

Northwestern FSAE Undertray

Year2024
RoleDesigner
ToolsAnsys Fluent · SolidWorks

Project Overview

Design and manufacture an undertray that maximizes downforce and guides turbulent air away from the car.

Key Takeaways

As a car enthusiast, combining my love for mechanical design and automotive was an incredibly fulfilling experience. I was able to see a direct correlation between my fluids classwork and practical industry applications.

I also gained exposure to simulation software, specifically CFD, through Ansys Fluent. The undertray also allowed me to further hone my skills in CAD (SolidWorks).

Design Considerations

At the start of the design process, I explored variations in the undertray geometry to evaluate their impact on downforce. Key parameters included ride height, inlet and outlet angles, and overall length. To study these effects, I developed five distinct undertray configurations, using the previous year's design (V1) as a baseline.

These five distinct designs were then run through a 2D CFD simulation in Ansys Fluent to determine the optimal parameters.

Looking at the L/D ratios, V4 yielded the best results. I utilized the inlet and outlet degrees to guide our final design.

The final design was run through CFD to validate the effectiveness of the undertray with the car moving at 30 mph.
