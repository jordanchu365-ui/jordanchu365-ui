About

I am a Junior at Northwestern University pursuing a BS in mechanical engineering with a concentration in design.

I'm especially interested in technical product design and creating solutions that are both functional and human-centered. I love working hands-on, whether that's building prototypes, machining parts, or exploring ideas through 3D design. I've had the chance to work in both industry and research, and those experiences have shaped how I approach engineering: iterate quickly, test often, and design with the user in mind.

I'm also drawn to where engineering meets business. Through the Kellogg School of Management's Certificate Program for Undergraduates (CPU) in Managerial Analytics, I'm building the analytical and strategic side of my toolkit — because the best products come from teams that understand cost, manufacturability, and the customer as deeply as the mechanics. Long term, I'm excited by roles that bridge both worlds, from design engineering through to product and project management.

June – Sept 2024 Columbus, Ohio

Research Intern

Hoelzle Research Lab

At the Hoelzle Research Lab, I worked alongside PhD students on trilinear coordinate scanning research for minimally invasive surgical applications. I generated 3D surfaces using MATLAB, prepared models for CT scanning, and converted CT scan data into Bezier surfaces using Jafari and Gans approximation. I also programmed a printer to follow these virtual point clouds and print directly onto surfaces, mimicking surgical toolpaths. Our work was presented at SFFS 2024, and the experience gave me exposure to advanced computational modeling and biomedical engineering workflows.
