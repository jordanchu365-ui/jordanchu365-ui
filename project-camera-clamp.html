MRD Camera Clamp

Year2024
RoleDesigner
ToolsFusion 360 · Polycarbonate

Project Overview

Design and manufacture a camera clamp for the Hoelzle Research MRD Printer.

Takeaways

This was my first project using 3D modeling software (Fusion 360). While the design itself was relatively simple, creating these components solidified my interest in mechanical design and introduced me to key considerations such as tolerancing and material selection.

The camera clamp was used to document over 30 hours of experimental trials, with footage presented at the 2024 Solid Freeform Fabrication Symposium. The clamp remains in active use today!

Design Considerations

The Hoelzle Research Lab utilizes an MRD printer that extrudes bio-gels. The previous camera clamp was fixed to the printer, which follows the extrusion nozzle. The previous clamp only allowed for one degree of freedom in adjustment, making for very poor video recording quality.

To support the weight of the camera while allowing for 3 degrees of freedom in adjustment, I decided to create a 3-piece clamp fixed to the existing mount to create a 4-bar linkage.

To minimize production costs while maintaining structural stability, the camera clamp was printed in polycarbonate.
